Department of Aerospace Engineering

Established in 1969, the Department of Aerospace Engineering stands at the forefront of fundamental and applied research and development, with a keen focus on scientific and social impact in the country. The department has contributed to manpower training via graduate research programs (PhD and Master of Science by research) as well as course-based programs (M.Tech, Dual Degree and B.Tech).

Areas of Research

  • Aerodynamics
  • Flight Mechanics
  • Aerospace Propulsion
  • Aerospace Structures

Department of Applied Mechanics

Since its inception in 1959, the Department of Applied Mechanics has grown into a full-fledged interdisciplinary graduate research department. Focusing on academic undertakings in Biomedical Engineering, Fluid Mechanics and Solid Mechanics, the department boasts state-of-the-art facilities, a new Direct PhD program that has attracted many bright minds to pursue research as a career, as well as international recognition for their industrial research and sponsored projects.

Areas of Research

  • Solid Mechanics
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Biomedical Engineering

Department of Biotechnology

The Department of Biotechnology was founded in 2004 with a strong interdisciplinary research and teaching base in biological sciences and engineering. It carries out collaborative research with other departments within the Institute as well as other local and international institutes. Recently, the department also received significant funding and set up a cutting-edge facility for biotechnology research.

About the Department

The Department of Biotechnology is housed in two adjacent multi-storied buildings, one being the Bhupat and Jyoti Mehta School of Biosciences. The department, though relatively young but has seen rapid growth in the past 10 years.

The perfect blend of engineers and hardcore scientists contributes to the highly interdisciplinary and heterogeneous culture of the department. Students of this department have been successful in getting foreign internships which helps them to explore distinct fields in Biotechnology.

Areas of Research

  • Chemical Biology
  • Computational Biology
  • Biological Engineering
  • Biological Sciences

Department of Chemical Engineering

Aiming to be a global centre of academic and research excellence in chemical engineering sciences and technology, the Department of Chemical Engineering has seen a dramatic increase in industrial collaboration and consultancy, research projects, research publications and faculty awards. Currently, it is establishing world-class facilities and research groups in the areas of energy, environment and materials.

About the Department

Chemical engineering traditionally deals with how a chemical plant operates, but today it covers a wide range of areas like biotechnology, catalysis, pollution control, renewable energy such as solar energy, drug delivery, process control and systems engineering, microelectronics, polymers, surfactants, systems and computational biology, reactor designing, heat and mass transfer and many more.

Chemical engineering is a broad-based and interdisciplinary field and provides opportunities in several industries - programming, biology, or even economics; a flexibility that is not easily available in other disciplines.

Department of Chemistry

The Department of Chemistry is one of the first to function after IIT Madras was founded in 1959. Since then, the faculty has set up three different research centres for nanoscience, catalysis research as well as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and spectroscopy. Today, the department stands among the best in India for teaching and research, and is well recognized across the world through its Alumni. Several alumni have excelled in their chosen professions and many have been recognised by IIT Madras as Distinguished Alumni.
